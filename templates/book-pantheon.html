Title Author(s) Brief Summary
The Master and the Margarita Mikhail Bulgakov A comedic, surrealistic story of devil's visit to soviet Russia. Travel to 1930, explore Christianity/Atheism and train your creativity.
Crime and Punishment Fyodor Dostoevsky Realistic overview of how a crime is carried out and its impact on one's psychology. The author attempts to talk about morals, which according to Dostoevsky's point of view are found primarily in religion
The Grapes of Wrath John Steinbeck The Joads, a farm family, are forced to migrate due to extreme poverty. They are treated as inferiors by their own American people and are being exploited. The book circles around the themes of police brutality, social injustice, racism, sexism, religion and communism.
The Trial Franz Kafka Kafka's "The Trial" stories the life of an individual that is mysteriously asked to appear in court according to absolutely no evidence and reason. It states the powerlessness of the individual while being faced with a totalitarian, corrupt and non-democratic government.
Brave New World & 1984 Aldous Huxley & George Orwell

I decided to include these books into a single entry, but I would definitely recommend reading them both! They attempt to predict the possibly dystopian future of our planet.


It's incredible and perhaps daunting how many of their concepts appear into our modern daily lives. Pointless wars, soma (addicting substances/habits which make us ignore the world's real problems, monitor our behavior/protesting and instead provide us with temporary and artificial pleasure as a form of blindness (e.g. mobile phones, social media)), sacrifice of freedom for stability, totalitarianism and public opinion control
